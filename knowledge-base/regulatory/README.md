# Regulatory Framework 📋

This directory contains regulatory requirements and compliance guidelines by sector.

## Purpose

- Help founders understand regulatory landscape
- Identify compliance requirements early
- Highlight sector-specific red flags
- Guide sector selection based on founder's risk tolerance

## Key Regulators in India

| Regulator | Acronym | Focus |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Reserve Bank of India | RBI | Banking, payments, lending |
| Securities & Exchange Board | SEBI | Investments, securities |
| Ministry of Electronics & IT | MeitY | Tech, startups, e-governance |
| Ministry of Corporate Affairs | MCA | Company law, compliance |
| Food Safety Authority | FSSAI | Food/beverage startups |
| Income Tax Authority | ITA | Tax compliance |
| Income Tax Department | ITD | Direct taxation |

## Red Flags by Sector

### FinTech
- ❌ Handling customer money without proper licenses
- ❌ Cross-border remittance without RBI approval
- ❌ Lending without NBFC registration (where required)
- ❌ Crypto without regulatory clarity

### HealthTech
- ❌ Unlicensed doctors providing consultations
- ❌ Selling prescription medicines without verification
- ❌ Unverified medical claims
- ❌ Patient data breaches

### AgriTech
- ❌ Buying/selling without APMC registration (where required)
- ❌ Unverified agricultural claims
- ❌ Exploitative pricing of farmers

### eCommerce
- ❌ Inventory model violating FDI caps
- ❌ No clear refund policy
- ❌ Unverified sellers on marketplace

## Green Signals

### All Sectors
✅ Early regulatory consultation  
✅ Clear compliance roadmap  
✅ Legal entity setup (Pvt Ltd or LLP)  
✅ DPIIT recognition (for eligible startups)  

### FinTech
✅ RBI Sandbox participation  
✅ Banking partner relationships  
✅ Clear data security protocols  

### HealthTech
✅ Licensed practitioner partnerships  
✅ HIPAA-like data security  
✅ Clear consent forms  

### AgriTech
✅ Fair pricing commitment  
✅ Farmer data protection  
✅ Government partnership (DAC, PM-AASHA)  

## Government Support

### Startup India Scheme
- **Eligibility**: Incorporated within 7 years, innovation focus
- **Benefits**: 
  - 3-year tax exemption on profits
  - Reduced compliance requirements
  - Priority government procurement
  - Access to funding through SEBI-registered funds

### PLI (Production Linked Incentive)
- Sectors: Electronics, telecom, pharma, auto, textiles
- Benefits: 4-6% cash subsidy on incremental production

### Digital India Initiative
- Support for digital products and services
- Grants and infrastructure support
