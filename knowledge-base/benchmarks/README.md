# Benchmarks & KPIs 📊

This directory contains industry benchmarks and key performance indicators for Indian startups.

## Purpose

- Compare your startup metrics against industry standards
- Identify if your unit economics are healthy
- Benchmark growth rates against similar startups
- Use as reference for fundraising conversations

## Key Files

### `unit-economics.json`
Detailed unit economics benchmarks by sector:
- **SaaS**: CAC payback 6-12 months, LTV:CAC 3:1+, NRR 100-130%
- **EdTech**: CAC ₹1-3K, LTV:CAC 2:1-5:1, Churn 3-8% monthly
- **eCommerce**: CAC ₹200-800, Repeat rate 20-40%, Take rate 15-25%
- **FinTech**: CAC varies by subsector, Payback 2-12 months
- **HealthTech**: CAC ₹300-1K, Repeat rate 30-50%

## Healthy Unit Economics Checklist

### Essential Metrics
- [ ] LTV:CAC ratio ≥ 3:1
- [ ] CAC payback period ≤ 12 months
- [ ] Monthly churn < 5-8%
- [ ] Repeat purchase rate > 20%
- [ ] Gross margins > 50%

### Stage-Specific Benchmarks

| Metric | Pre-Seed | Seed | Series A | Series B |
|--------|----------|------|----------|----------|
| **Revenue** | $0-50K | $50K-500K | $500K-2M | $2M-10M |
| **Growth** | MoM | 100%+ YoY | 50-100% YoY | 40-50% YoY |
| **CAC Payback** | <18 months | <12 months | <12 months | <12 months |
| **LTV:CAC** | 2:1+ | 3:1+ | 3:1+ | 4:1+ |

## By-Sector Deep Dive

### 🏦 SaaS
**Healthy Benchmarks:**
- Gross margin: 75-85%
- Magic Number: 0.75-1.0 (ARR growth / S&M spend)
- Rule of 40: Growth % + Profit margin % > 40
- NRR (Net Revenue Retention): 100-130%

**Red Flags:**
- Gross margin < 60%
- Magic Number < 0.5
- NRR < 100% (churn too high)

### 📚 EdTech
**Healthy Benchmarks:**
- CAC: ₹1-3K (high competition)
- LTV: ₹5-15K
- Churn: 3-8% monthly
- Repeat rate: 20-40%

**Red Flags:**
- CAC > ₹3K without strong retention
- Churn > 10% monthly
- Gross margin < 60%
- Profitability path unclear

### 🛒 eCommerce/Marketplace
**Healthy Benchmarks:**
- CAC: ₹200-800 (lower than SaaS)
- Repeat purchase: 20-40%
- Take rate: 15-25%
- Gross margin: 35-50%

**Red Flags:**
- Repeat rate < 15%
- Take rate too thin (< 10%)
- Operating margin negative after 3 years

### 💰 FinTech
**Healthy Benchmarks:**
- CAC payback: 2-12 months (subsector dependent)
- Monthly churn: 1-3%
- Profitability: Faster than other sectors

**Red Flags:**
- Regulatory compliance costs unclear
- Unit economics dependent on volume

## India-Specific Considerations

### Higher CAC Reality
- CAC in India is 2-3x higher than developed markets
- Competition in emerging sectors is intense
- Customer acquisition through marketing is expensive
- Partnership/organic channels are critical

### Profitability Timeline
- Most startups take 5-7 years to profitability in India
- Unit economics must be strong before scaling
- Limited VC capital means efficiency is critical
- Path to profitability should be clear by Series A

### Segment-Specific Variations
- Tier 1 cities: Lower CAC, higher LTV
- Tier 2-3 cities: Higher CAC, lower LTV, longer payback
- Rural: Very high CAC, difficult retention
- Hybrid models (online + offline) show better unit economics

## Using Benchmarks in Validation

### Validation Scoring
Your metrics are compared against sector benchmarks:
- **Excellent**: Top 20% of sector
- **Good**: 50-80th percentile
- **Fair**: 20-50th percentile
- **Weak**: Bottom 20%

### Red Flags Detected
- LTV:CAC < 2:1 → Major concern
- Churn > 10% → Retention problem
- Payback > 18 months → Scaling risk
- No profitability path → Sustainability concern
